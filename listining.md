## 1. What are the steps/strategies to do Active Listening?
- Give your full attention to the speaker and avoid distractions.
- Show that you are listening through eye contact, nodding, and open body posture.
- Do not interrupt—wait for the speaker to finish completely.
- Paraphrase or summarize to check if you understood their message correctly.
- Ask questions to clarify anything that seems unclear.
- Respond thoughtfully and respectfully, acknowledging both their words and their feelings.
- Stay patient and keep an open mind without judging or jumping to conclusions.
## 2. According to Fisher's model, what are the key points of Reflective Listening? 
- Listen carefully to understand what the speaker really means, not just the words.
- Repeat or restate what the speaker said to check if you understood them correctly.
- Notice the speaker’s emotions and reflect them back (e.g., “You sound worried”).
- Ask gentle questions when something is unclear so you fully understand the message.
- Avoid judging, arguing, or giving fast solutions—focus on understanding first.
## 3. What are the obstacles in your listening process?
- Getting distracted by my phone, noise, or other activities.
- Thinking about what I will say next instead of listening fully.
- Feeling tired, stressed, or emotionally disturbed.
- Losing interest when the topic feels long or not relevant to me.
- Failing to notice the speaker’s tone, expressions, or body language.
## 4. What can you do to improve your listening?
- Remove distractions and stay focused on the speaker.
- Maintain eye contact and use positive body language.
- Allow the speaker to finish before I respond.
- Ask questions when something is unclear.
- Paraphrase or summarize important points to confirm understanding.
- Stay calm and control emotional reactions.
## 5.When do you switch to Passive communication style in your day-to-day life?
- When I want to avoid conflict or keep peace in a situation.
- When I feel shy, nervous, or unsure of my opinion.
- When the other person seems dominant or aggressive.
- When I think my ideas won’t be valued or taken seriously.
- When I don’t want to hurt someone’s feelings.
## 6.When do you switch into Aggressive communication style in your day-to-day life?
- When I am angry, frustrated, or under stress.
- When I strongly disagree and feel the need to “win” the argument.
- When I feel disrespected or ignored.
- When I am in a hurry and want things done my way.
- When I feel certain that I am right and dismiss others’ opinions.
## 7.When do you switch into Passive-Aggressive communication style in your day-to-day life?
- When I feel hurt or ignored but don’t want to express it openly.
- When I want to avoid a direct fight but still want the other person to know I’m upset.
- When I don’t feel comfortable speaking honestly about my feelings.
- When I want to maintain the relationship but still feel angry inside.
- When I expect the other person to “figure out” my feelings without me saying it directly.
## 8.How can you make your communication assertive?
- Speak clearly and respectfully about what I think, feel, and need.
- Use a calm and steady tone, not too soft and not too harsh.
- Maintain good posture and eye contact to show confidence.
- Use “I” statements (e.g., “I feel…,” “I need…”) instead of blaming others.
- Set boundaries politely — learn to say “no” without guilt.
- Listen to others and try to understand their viewpoint before responding.
