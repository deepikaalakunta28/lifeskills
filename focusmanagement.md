### 1. What is Deep Work?
Deep Work is the act of focusing without distraction on a cognitively demanding task.
Cal Newport defines it as:
- Professional activities performed in a state of distraction-free concentration
- That push your cognitive abilities to their limit
- Create new value, improve your skills, and are hard to replicate
### 2.According to author how to do deep work properly, in a few points?
#### Work deeply
- Set a fixed place and time for deep work.
- Remove distractions (phone away, browser tabs closed).
#### Embrace boredom
- Train your mind to stay focused instead of seeking entertainment.
- Take breaks without switching to social media.
#### Quit or limit social media
- Keep only the platforms that directly support your goals.
- Reduce mindless scrolling.
#### Drain the shallow
- Reduce time spent on low-value tasks like constant emailing.
- Set boundaries, use schedules, and say “no” more often.
### 3.How can you implement the principles in your day to day life?
- Start with one 45–60 minute deep work session per day.
- Plan each session: what exactly will you do, and what is the goal?
- Use time blocks to protect your deep work hours.
- Turn off notifications and keep your phone outside your workspace.
- Put shallow tasks at fixed times, e.g., emails only at 12 pm and 4 pm.
- End your day with a shutdown routine, so your brain can rest.
### 4.What are the dangers of social media, in brief?
#### Weakens your ability to focus
- Constant switching reduces mental stamina and deep thinking ability.
#### Highly addictive
- Algorithms reward your brain with dopamine, making quitting difficult.
#### Wastes enormous time
- Small “checks” add up to hours lost every day.
#### Creates anxiety and comparison
- Seeing curated lives makes your own life seem less satisfying.
#### Affects sleep and mental health
- Screens overstimulate the brain before bedtime.
