### 1.What are the activities you do that make you relax - Calm quadrant?
- Gentle walks or being in nature
- Listening to soft music
- Meditation or breathwork
- Taking a warm shower/bath
- Gardening
- Spending quiet time with pets
### 2.When do you find getting into the Stress quadrant?
- Tight deadlines
- Learning something new that feels overwhelming
- Social pressure or conflict
- Multitasking or handling too many responsibilities
### 3.How do you understand if you are in the Excitement quadrant?
- You feel energized and motivated
- You’re deeply engaged or in “flow”
- Tasks feel challenging but doable
- You feel optimistic and curious
### 4.Paraphrase the Sleep is your Superpower video in your own words in brief. Only the points, no explanation?
- Sleep strengthens memory
- Sleep boosts creativity
- Lack of sleep harms the brain
- Sleep improves immune function
- Sleep affects emotional regulation
### 5.What are some ideas that you can implement to sleep better?
- Keep a consistent sleep schedule
- Limit screen time at least 1 hour before bed
- Create a dark, cool, quiet sleep environment
- Avoid caffeine late in the day
- Reduce heavy meals close to bedtime
### 6.Paraphrase the video - Brain Changing Benefits of Exercise. Minimum 5 points, only the points?
- Exercise increases brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF)
- Exercise enhances learning and memory
- It boosts mood by increasing neurotransmitters like serotonin and dopamine
- Exercise reduces stress and anxiety levels
- It protects the brain from aging and neurodegeneration
### 7.What are some steps you can take to exercise more?
- Set small, achievable workout goals (e.g., 10 minutes daily)
- Schedule exercise like an appointment
- Choose activities you enjoy (dancing, walking, cycling, sports)
- Keep workout clothes and gear easily accessible
- Incorporate movement into daily life (walk breaks, stairs, stretching)
