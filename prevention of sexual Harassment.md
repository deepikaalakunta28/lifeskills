### 1. What kinds of behaviour cause sexual harassment?
- Unwelcome physical contact such as touching, hugging, or brushing against someone
- Requests or demands for sexual favours, directly or indirectly
- Sexually coloured remarks, jokes, comments, or innuendos
- Showing or sharing pornographic or sexually explicit content
- Unwanted staring, leering, or making sexual gestures
- Sending inappropriate messages, emails, or calls of a sexual nature
- Repeated teasing, bullying, or intimidation with sexual undertones
- Any unwelcome conduct that creates a hostile, uncomfortable, or offensive work environment
### 2. What would you do in case you face or witness any incident or repeated incidents of such behaviour?
- Clearly tell the person to stop, if it is safe to do so
- Record details of the incident(s): date, time, place, and witnesses
- Report the incident to the Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) or as per company PoSH policy
- Inform HR or a senior authority if ICC details are not known
- Support and encourage the affected person to report if you are a witness
- Avoid blaming the victim and maintain confidentiality
- Cooperate with any investigation process
- Follow up if the behaviour continues or if no action is taken
